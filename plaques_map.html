What are the Blue Plaques?

Roger Daltrey and Pete Townsend unveil a Blue Plaque commemorating the recording of 'The Who - Live At Leeds' at Leeds University Refectory. [Source: The BBC]

Blue Plaques are signs erected in public places, that commemorate historic events associated with that location, and the lives of historic people who have had an association with the location and typically they have lived in the building to which the plaque is attached. The earliest and most widely established scheme is run in London and is now administered by English Heritage; separately administered schemes are run in many other parts of the country. Like many cities, Leeds has a Blue Plaque scheme, with over 100 plaques designated. The map is showing 107 Blue Plaque locations in Leeds.
